# breast-cancer-luminal-continuum
Undergraduate capstone project investigating subtype ambiguity between Luminal A and Luminal B breast cancer tumors.

**Project Overview**

This repository contains the code, figures, and processed results used for my undergraduate capstone thesis project investigating subtype ambiguity between Luminal A and Luminal B breast cancer tumors.

Breast cancer is a heterogeneous disease composed of multiple molecular subtypes with distinct biological and clinical characteristics. While Luminal A and Luminal B tumors are traditionally treated as separate subtypes, gene expression patterns suggest that some tumors may exhibit characteristics of both groups. The goal of this project was to investigate whether these tumors represent biologically meaningful intermediate states along a molecular continuum rather than strictly distinct categories.

Using transcriptomic data from The Cancer Genome Atlas (TCGA-BRCA), I applied machine learning, dimensionality reduction, pathway enrichment analysis, and survival analysis to characterize ambiguous tumors and evaluate their biological significance.

**Research Question**

Do Luminal A and Luminal B breast cancer tumors exist as completely distinct molecular subtypes, or do some tumors represent intermediate transitional states between the two groups?

**Methods**

_1. Dimensionality Reduction_

Principal Component Analysis (PCA) and Uniform Manifold Approximation and Projection (UMAP) were used to visualize molecular relationships among breast cancer subtypes.

Purpose:
- Explore subtype clustering patterns
- Identify overlap between Luminal A and Luminal B tumors
- Detect potential intermediate tumor states

_2. _Machine_ Learning Classification_

A Support Vector Machine (SVM) classifier was trained to distinguish Luminal A and Luminal B tumors using gene expression profiles.

Purpose:
- Evaluate subtype separability
- Generate prediction probabilities
- Identify tumors with ambiguous classifications
  
_3. Gene Signature Analysis_

Gene expression signatures associated with Luminal A and Luminal B tumors were examined to determine whether ambiguous tumors displayed mixed molecular characteristics.

Purpose:
- Quantify subtype similarity
- Identify intermediate molecular patterns

_4. Pathway Enrichment Analysis__

Differentially expressed genes from ambiguous tumors were analyzed using pathway enrichment approaches.

Purpose:
- Identify biological processes associated with ambiguity
- Characterize proliferation, hormone signaling, migration, and chromatin-related pathways
  
_5. Survival Analysis_

Kaplan–Meier survival analysis and log-rank testing were performed to compare clinical outcomes among Luminal A, Luminal B, and ambiguous tumor groups.

Purpose:
Determine whether ambiguous tumors exhibit intermediate clinical behavior

**Main Findings**

PCA and UMAP revealed substantial overlap between Luminal A and Luminal B tumors.
The SVM classifier achieved strong overall performance but identified tumors with intermediate prediction probabilities.
Ambiguous tumors displayed biological characteristics associated with both Luminal A and Luminal B subtypes.
Pathway enrichment analysis revealed mixed enrichment patterns involving cell cycle regulation, hormone signaling, chromosome segregation, and migration-related pathways.
Survival analysis suggested that ambiguous tumors exhibit clinical behavior intermediate between clearly classified Luminal A and Luminal B tumors.
These findings support the hypothesis that some breast tumors may exist along a molecular continuum rather than fitting into completely discrete subtype categories.
